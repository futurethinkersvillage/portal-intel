# PORTAL INTEL — CONTENT STANDARDS

> This document is the single source of truth for what belongs in the Portal Intel feed.
> It is injected into every AI enrichment and extraction prompt. Edit this file to tune
> the system — no code changes required.
>
> Last updated: 2026-04-10

---

## MISSION

Surface actionable, time-sensitive intelligence for people actively building resilient rural lives and communities in British Columbia, Alberta, and key international markets (Argentina, Colombia). Every item in the feed must help someone make a real decision this week — about land, money, labor, risk, or strategy.

---

## THE AUDIENCE

Operators, investors, and builders with skin in the game. They are:
- Buying, developing, or actively managing rural/off-grid land in BC, AB, or internationally
- Building or investing in resilience infrastructure: off-grid power, water systems, food production, rural housing
- Running or scaling rural hospitality, agritourism, retreat centers, or community ventures
- Seeking grants, skilled trades labor, or policy intelligence to execute real projects
- Making $100K+ decisions based on what they find here

They are NOT: tourists, hobbyists, dreamers researching "someday-maybe" lifestyles, Vancouver urbanites looking for weekend getaways, or government employees browsing job boards.

---

## THE TEST

Before including any item, ask: **Can someone take a concrete action on this within 30 days?**

A qualifying item has at least ONE of:
- A price, acreage, dollar amount, or wage
- A deadline or event date (that has not already passed)
- A direct URL to apply, buy, register, or act
- A regulatory change with a specific effective date
- A named person or organization to contact

If none of these are present, the item is informational filler. Score it 0.0 and exclude it.

## CATEGORIES & WHAT QUALIFIES

### LAND — Rural BC/AB Property (highest priority, score 0.6–1.0)

INCLUDE:
- Rural properties with acreage + price + location + at minimum one of: water source, road access, power, existing structures
- Off-grid homesteads, farms, ranches, retreat centers, campgrounds, eco-lodges for sale
- Crown land opportunities, land auctions, ALR properties, properties with mineral or water rights
- Land with clear development potential: zoning details, permit status, road access status
- Operational rural businesses for sale: working campgrounds, farms, lodges, retreat centers

EXAMPLE headlines that qualify:
- "160-acre riverfront acreage near Clinton, BC — $399K, 2 cabins, year-round road"
- "78-acre self-sufficient estate, Seymour Arm BC — $848K, log home, greenhouse, off-grid"
- "2,277-acre ranch, Clinton BC — $6.995M, 12 titles, range permit, irrigation water"
- "Cherryville Gold Panner campground for sale — established operation, river access"

EXCLUDE:
- Ski cabin lots (recreational, not resilience)
- Luxury estates over $3M with no operational purpose
- Property market trend maps or search portals (not actual listings)
- Properties with no price, no acreage, or no location
- BC Assessment news, employer rankings, or non-listing content

### INTL-LAND — International Land (Argentina & Colombia primary, score 0.6–1.0)

INCLUDE:
- Resorts, retreat centers, eco-lodges, and rural land for sale in Argentina and Colombia
- Must have: price in USD, property size or acreage, location (region/department), and a way to inquire
- Operational properties preferred: working farms, functioning lodges, established eco-tourism operations
- Raw land only if it has road access, water, and is in a viable rural area

EXAMPLE headlines that qualify:
- "12-hectare eco-resort, Valle de Cocora Colombia — $1.2M USD, 8 cabins, spring water, operational"
- "65-acre cattle farm + guesthouse, Mendoza Argentina — $380K USD, water rights, road access"
- "3-hectare beachfront eco-lodge site, Caribbean Colombia — $220K USD, permit-ready"

EXCLUDE:
- Urban condos, tourist apartments, timeshares
- Properties with no USD price or no location
- Non-operational raw land with no access or infrastructure

### GRANTS — Funding Programs (high priority, score 0.6–1.0)

INCLUDE:
- Specific programs with dollar amounts AND eligibility criteria AND either a deadline or open intake
- Rural development, agri-food, renewable energy, workforce training, Indigenous economic development
- BC/AB provincial programs, NDIT, FCC, AAFC, BCAC, BCEFA, federal rural programs

EXAMPLE headlines that qualify:
- "BC Employer Training Grant — up to $300K for skills training, rolling intake, apply online"
- "Alberta Rural Grant Program — up to $100K for property development, deadline June 30"
- "4 Okanagan manufacturers share $6.75M provincial fund — next intake window open"

EXCLUDE:
- Generic government portals ("Search funding & grants", "Find Support and Make Connections")
- ESG/DEI-specific programs or Indigenous reconciliation programs with no land/build relevance
- Programs with no dollar amount AND no deadline AND no eligibility specifics
- "StrongerBC" or other government branding pages with no actionable program details
- Visit the ESG Centre of Excellence — exclude always

### RISKS — Policy & Environmental Threats (high priority, score 0.6–1.0)

INCLUDE:
- Active wildfires, flood events, or emergency orders affecting BC/AB rural areas — with location and size
- Policy changes affecting land use, taxation, carbon costs, water rights, ALR rules, rural business — with timeline
- Regulatory updates for construction, trades, or rural business with compliance deadlines
- US tariffs, trade policy, or cross-border changes with direct material impact on rural BC/AB operators

EXAMPLE headlines that qualify:
- "550-hectare wildfire east of Merritt — evacuation alert expanded, April 9"
- "Clearwater & Barriere OCPs updated March 2026 — new zoning rules for rural development"
- "Alberta WCB 2026 premium rates published — budget your workforce costs now"
- "BC carbon tax schedule changes — rural fuel user impacts effective July 1"

EXCLUDE:
- Local crime reports (theft, assault, drug seizures) — not strategic risk intel
- Vancouver or Victoria-area policy with no rural BC application
- Historical commemorations or feel-good public safety stories
- High-risk offender notifications (not relevant to this audience)
- Minor traffic or weather incidents

### JOBS — Trades & Rural Work (medium priority, score 0.5–0.8)

INCLUDE:
- Trades directly relevant to off-grid/rural builds: electricians, plumbers, pipefitters, heavy equipment operators, carpenters, construction supervisors, gas fitters, excavators, lineworkers
- Agricultural roles with wages: farm supervisors, farm workers, market garden workers — in BC or AB only
- Rural hospitality operations: campground managers, lodge operators, retreat center staff
- Must be in BC or AB. Must include a wage/salary range. Must have a direct application path.

EXAMPLE headlines that qualify:
- "Heavy Equipment Operator, Fort McMurray AB — $37–43/hr, apply via Job Bank"
- "Plumber Apprentice, Revelstoke BC — $25–40/hr, Velocity Plumbing & Heating"
- "Farm Supervisor, Nakusp BC — $25/hr campground attendant, seasonal"

EXCLUDE (these are hard rules, no exceptions):
- Jobs in Ontario, Quebec, Nova Scotia, Manitoba, Northwest Territories, or any province outside BC/AB
- Nursing, clinical health, home support, childcare, elder care
- Food service: barista, line cook, restaurant manager, food services supervisor
- Retail: grocery clerk, store supervisor, sales associate
- General office/admin: bookkeeper (unless rural operator context), marketing coordinator
- Jobs with no wage listed
- Generic BC Public Service portal pages and category listings (not actual postings)
- Federal Job Bank listings without verified BC/AB location

### EVENTS — Workshops & Trade Shows (medium priority, high bar, score 0.5–0.8)

INCLUDE:
- Workshops with direct skills relevance: water systems, solar/off-grid power, permaculture, construction, agri-food, land stewardship, rural business
- Trade shows for builders and rural operators: home shows, agricultural expos, construction expos, metalworking events
- Regional gatherings with genuine networking value for rural operators in Interior BC or Alberta
- Must have a future date, a location, and a way to register or attend

EXAMPLE headlines that qualify:
- "Water Management Workshop — Rainway Project, April 12, free, St George Vancouver"
- "Kamloops Spring Home Show — April 11–12, exhibitors and products for builders"
- "MME Langley 2026 — Metalworking, Fabricating & Welding, free admission, April 9"
- "UBCM 2026 annual conference — all BC mayors, CAOs, regional directors, September"

EXCLUDE (hard rules):
- Music concerts, comedy shows, galas, raves, nightclubs, DJ events
- Vancouver/Victoria/urban cultural events with no rural operator relevance
- Online webinars on generic topics: investing, psychology, ancestry research, programming
- Sports events, tournaments, athletic competitions
- Pet adoption stories, school events, Easter/holiday community events
- Tourism events (winery tastings, ski resort parties, whale watching)
- Past events (event date has already passed — do not include)
- UBC Okanagan or university campus events (scavenger hunts, giving days, etc.)

### COMMUNITY — Local Operators & Initiatives (lower priority, high bar, score 0.5–0.7)

INCLUDE:
- Local operators doing something genuinely relevant: a new rural business, a community land initiative, a co-op forming, a shared infrastructure win
- Policy advocacy affecting rural BC/AB: construction association positions on legislation, rural infrastructure funding, Indigenous land rights wins
- Must have a concrete action: a way to get involved, partner, invest, contact, or follow up

EXAMPLE headlines that qualify:
- "Cherryville family acquires Gold Panner campground — open to partnerships, contact details"
- "7 new first responders trained in Barriere — growing rural emergency capacity"
- "ACA calls for infrastructure certainty in Alberta Budget 2026 — submission open for comment"

EXCLUDE (hard rules):
- Feel-good stories with no action or contact path
- Music festival announcements or entertainment coverage
- Obituaries under any circumstances
- Sports scores and athletic achievements
- General local interest (egg hunts, bowling leagues, pet stories)
- Chamber events with no specific rural operator relevance
- Duplicate stories (same event covered twice by different sources)

### BUY/SELL — Equipment & Infrastructure (lower priority, score 0.5–0.75)

INCLUDE:
- Equipment with direct rural operations relevance: solar panels, batteries, generators, water tanks, ATVs, farm machinery, tractors, trailers, fencing, livestock equipment, building materials
- Vehicles relevant to rural operations: trucks, utility vehicles, RVs for land use
- Tools for construction, farming, or off-grid infrastructure
- Must have: price, condition, and location in BC or AB

EXAMPLE headlines that qualify:
- "10kW solar array + battery bank, BC Interior — $12,000 OBO, complete system"
- "2019 Kubota BX tractor, 200 hrs, attachments — $24,500, Kamloops"
- "Used water storage tanks (5,000L), Kelowna BC — $800 each, pickup only"

EXCLUDE:
- Bicycle parts and cycling equipment of any kind (hard rule, always exclude)
- Consumer electronics, clothing, household items
- Generic classified ads with no rural operations relevance
- Items located outside BC or AB

## AUTOMATIC EXCLUSIONS — SCORE 0.0, ARCHIVE IMMEDIATELY

No matter how the headline is framed, these are always excluded:

**Content types:**
- Anything about bicycles: wheelsets, pedals, saddles, derailleurs, handlebars, cassettes, cranksets, forks, stems, grips, bike tubes, bike tires — always 0.0
- Tourism marketing: "Exploring BC", "Top Places to Spot", "Where to See", "Things to do in", "Bucket List", "5 Ways to Get From"
- Obituaries, memorial notices, tributes
- Sports scores, game results, athletic achievements
- Local crime briefs: theft reports, assault, drug seizures, sex offences (unless it is a major policy-level risk to rural communities)
- Pet stories, animal adoption, wildlife festival fluff
- Easter/holiday events, birthday parties, community socials
- Online quizzes, puzzles, trivia
- Letters to the editor
- Generic "here's what's happening this weekend" community calendars
- Events where the date has already passed

**Geographic scope violations:**
- Jobs located outside BC or AB (Ontario, Quebec, Nova Scotia, MB, SK, territories, etc.)
- Urban Vancouver/Victoria entertainment with no rural relevance
- Anything about the Lower Mainland that isn't a policy or grant applicable region-wide

**Source quality indicators (auto-exclude):**
- Any item from a BC/AB tourism board or travel guide
- Any item from a nightlife/entertainment events platform
- Any item that is a search interface rather than a specific program or listing
- Any item from Kijiji that is not farm equipment, building materials, or rural operations equipment

## SOURCE QUALITY STANDARDS

A good source produces 3+ qualifying items per scrape. A source that produces 0 qualifying items in 3 consecutive scrapes should be disabled.

**Target source types:**
- Rural land listing aggregators: Landquest, FarmLink, PEI rural land, rural Kijiji real estate (not classifieds), Royal LePage rural
- BC/AB provincial grant program pages (specific program URLs, not homepages)
- Regional newspapers covering Interior BC: Clearwater Times, Barriere Star Journal, Merritt Herald, Williams Lake Tribune, Smithers Interior News, Prince George Citizen
- Alberta rural coverage: Grande Prairie Herald, Hinton Parklander, Rocky Mountain House Mountaineer
- Government alert feeds: BC Wildfire Service, Emergency BC, FLNR, Alberta Emergency Management
- BC/AB construction and trades associations: BCCSA, Alberta Construction Association
- Agricultural organizations: BC Agriculture Council, Alberta Federation of Agriculture, FCC
- Rural hospitality: campground/resort listing platforms, FarmStay Canada, HI Canada rural properties
- International land: Colombian rural real estate portals (FincaRaiz, MetroCuadrado rural), Argentine farmland databases (Argenprop rural, Inmuebles24 rural), expat property platforms

**Sources to permanently disable:**
- Kijiji (general classifieds) — produces bike parts and consumer goods
- Hello BC / Tourism BC — produces tourism marketing
- Eventbrite without rural filter — produces urban entertainment
- BC Public Service Jobs homepage — produces category pages, not postings
- Federal Job Bank without BC/AB geographic filter — produces Ontario/Quebec jobs
- Any Eventbrite search page
- Any "Things to do" aggregator
